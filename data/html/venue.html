The Österreichische Akademie der Wissenschaften (Austrian Academy of Sciences; ÖAW) is recognized as a legal entity with special safeguarding by the Republic of Austria.
As outlined in the Academy's statutes, its fundamental objective is to advance both the sciences and humanities across all domains, with a particular emphasis on foundational research.
Presently, the academy manages 25 research institutes. In 2012, a restructuring initiative led to the delegation of certain institutes to universities and subsequent amalgamations.
The academy's institutes are categorized into two primary sections: one dedicated to mathematics and natural sciences (mathematisch-naturwissenschaftliche Klasse),
and the other focusing on humanities and social sciences (philosophisch-historische Klasse).

A component of ÖAW is the ACDH, which in 2015 transformed from the "Austrian Centre for Digital Humanities (ACDH)" within the Austrian Academy of Sciences into a research institute.
Its primary objective was to advance the humanities by employing digital techniques and tools across various academic domains.
Towards the end of 2019, the institute underwent a substantial reorganization that led to the formation of three key domains: Infrastructure and Services, Digital Humanities Research, and Cultural Heritage Research.
During this restructuring, several research groups became affiliated with the Institute.

Since January 1, 2020, the expanded "Austrian Centre for Digital Humanities and Cultural Heritage (ACDH-CH)" has amalgamated two core focuses of the Austrian Academy of Sciences into a single institute.
This institute pursues two main avenues: (a) conducting fundamental research in the humanities via long-term projects geared towards the advancement and safeguarding of cultural heritage,
and (b) engaging in research concerning the methodological and theoretical frameworks for digitally documenting, processing, investigating, and visualizing aspects of the digital humanities.
Within the ACDH-CH, these two pillars are deliberately designed to increasingly complement each other, thereby contributing to collaborative endeavors aimed at the rich reservoir of Europe's cultural legacy.
